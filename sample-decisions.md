# Working example of decisions.md in use.

---

## Decisions

### 2026-03-13 — Product Title
**Decision:** Title locked as "Lab-in-a-Box: Multiple AIs, One Team, Zero Code."
**Rationale:** "Multiple AIs" signals this isn't another single-model
tutorial. "One Team" sells the concept without explaining the mechanics.
"Zero Code" removes the biggest objection before the buyer asks.
**Alternatives considered:** "AI Team Playbook" — too generic.
"Multi-Model Workflow Guide" — too technical for the target buyer.
**Status:** Final

---

### 2026-03-13 — Pricing
**Decision:** Price at $29.99 on Gumroad.
**Rationale:** Comps on Gumroad for AI workflow products cluster
between $24-$34. Middle of that range signals value without
overreaching for a first launch. Can adjust after initial sales data.
**Alternatives considered:** $24.99 — leaves money on the table
if demand is strong. $34.99 — too close to the ceiling for an
unproven product.
**Status:** Revisable after first 30 days of sales

---

### 2026-03-12 — GitHub as Shared Memory
**Decision:** GitHub is the coordination layer. No other platforms.
**Rationale:** Free, reliable, version-controlled, accessible from
any device, works with both models. Competing solutions require
Supabase, MCP, and Vercel. Our one-service approach is the
differentiator.
**Alternatives considered:** Notion — requires account and
sharing setup. Google Drive — no version history. Dropbox —
unnecessary overhead.
**Status:** Final

---

### 2026-03-12 — Build Sequence
**Decision:** Ship Tier 1 manual workflow first. Automation deferred to update.
**Rationale:** Working system exists today. Automation adds complexity
and delays launch. AI landscape moves fast — a timely simple product
beats a delayed comprehensive one. Tier 2 and 3 become post-launch
updates once buyer feedback identifies real pain points.
**Alternatives considered:** Build all three tiers before launch —
rejected as over-engineering that risks missing the market window.
**Status:** Final
